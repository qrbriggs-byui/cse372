# Reading 3.1 – Facilitating and Participating in a Requirements Workshop  
### Multiple Choice Questions

---

**1.** What is the primary goal of a requirements workshop?  
A. To design the final system interface  
B. To define and clarify user and business needs  
C. To write detailed technical specifications  
D. To assign project tasks to developers  
**Answer:** B  

---

**2.** Why is facilitation important in a requirements workshop?  
A. It ensures one person dominates the discussion  
B. It helps keep discussions focused, fair, and productive  
C. It limits participation to only technical staff  
D. It prevents creative thinking among participants  
**Answer:** B  

---

**3.** According to the reading, what is one of the most underrated skills in a requirements workshop?  
A. Note-taking  
B. Time management  
C. Active listening  
D. Presentation skills  
**Answer:** C  

---

**4.** In the example of the food-ordering system, what misunderstanding did the team initially have?  
A. They thought users wanted fewer menu options  
B. They believed “ease of use” referred to layout design  
C. They assumed login security was not important  
D. They ignored stakeholder feedback entirely  
**Answer:** B  

---

**5.** What is the benefit of asking “why” when users describe a need or frustration?  
A. It helps uncover the real motivation or problem behind the request  
B. It allows the facilitator to challenge the user’s authority  
C. It reduces the amount of information to document  
D. It delays the workshop to gather more data later  
**Answer:** A  

---

**6.** What is *post-it clustering* used for in a workshop?  
A. To prioritize development tasks  
B. To group similar ideas or themes visually  
C. To evaluate system performance metrics  
D. To document final system requirements  
**Answer:** B  

---

**7.** Which of the following is the best description of a *user story*?  
A. A fictional narrative about the project  
B. A format that describes a user’s need, goal, and benefit  
C. A technical document written by engineers  
D. A storyboard used for interface design  
**Answer:** B  

---

**8.** How does *dot voting* help in a requirements workshop?  
A. It helps reach quick consensus on which ideas to prioritize  
B. It assigns ownership of tasks to individual participants  
C. It replaces the need for discussion and debate  
D. It identifies which stakeholders are most powerful  
**Answer:** A  

---

**9.** Why is it important to summarize key takeaways at the end of a workshop?  
A. To ensure everyone agrees on what was discussed and next steps  
B. To fill in unused time on the agenda  
C. To create more documentation for management  
D. To avoid scheduling another meeting  
**Answer:** A  

---

**10.** What overall attitude does the author recommend for effective participation in a requirements workshop?  
A. Strict authority and control  
B. Empathy, curiosity, and openness  
C. Speed and decisiveness  
D. Skepticism and independence  
**Answer:** B
