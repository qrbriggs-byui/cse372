# Quiz 2-4: How to Perform a Competitive Analysis Informed by Real User Insights

## Question 1
**Competitive analysis is primarily about comparing as many features as possible between products.**  
❌ **False** — It’s about understanding what matters most to users, not just listing features.

---

## Question 2
**User research should inform what you look for in a competitive analysis.**  
✅ **True** — Insights from interviews and observations guide where to focus your comparisons.

---

## Question 3
**Direct competitors are products that serve the same audience and solve the same problem.**  
✅ **True** — They target similar users with similar goals.

---

## Question 4
**Indirect competitors are irrelevant because they operate in a different industry.**  
❌ **False** — They can solve the same problem in different ways and provide useful ideas.

---

## Question 5
**A feature comparison matrix should focus on features that users actually care about.**  
✅ **True** — It becomes a user-centered scorecard rather than a simple checklist.

---

## Question 6
**Competitive analysis should only measure quantitative factors like speed and feature count.**  
❌ **False** — You should also assess user experience, emotion, and clarity.

---

## Question 7
**Observing how competitors make users feel can reveal important UX differences.**  
✅ **True** — Emotional tone, feedback style, and usability all affect preference.

---

## Question 8
**Finding gaps between user needs and competitor offerings can highlight innovation opportunities.**  
✅ **True** — That’s where differentiation and product value emerge.

---

## Question 9
**When presenting your findings, it’s best to include visuals, quotes, and concise recommendations.**  
✅ **True** — Visual summaries and clear insights make your analysis actionable.

---

## Question 10
**Competitive analysis is about copying your competitors’ best features to stay competitive.**  
❌ **False** — It’s about designing solutions that meet unmet user needs, not imitation.
