# Quiz 1-4: IEEE Standards & the Everyday Documents of Requirements

## Question 1
**What is the main benefit of following IEEE standards for requirements?**
- A. They make requirements longer and more complex
- B. They ensure requirements are clear, testable, and traceable
- C. They eliminate the need for documentation
- D. They replace communication between teams  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 2
**Which IEEE standard is most commonly associated with software requirements today?**
- A. ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148
- B. IEEE 802.11
- C. ISO 9001
- D. IEEE 754  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 3
**What is one quality of a "good" requirement according to IEEE guidance?**
- A. It can have multiple interpretations
- B. It’s as short as possible, even if vague
- C. It is verifiable and unambiguous
- D. It only applies to design teams  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 4
**What is the main purpose of a Business Requirements Document (BRD)?**
- A. To describe the software’s architecture
- B. To define business outcomes and constraints in business language
- C. To detail how data is stored and processed
- D. To list all user interface elements  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 5
**Which document contains functional, non-functional, and constraint-based requirements?**
- A. User Story
- B. Business Requirements Document (BRD)
- C. Software Requirements Specification (SRS)
- D. Use Case Diagram  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 6
**What is the main focus of user stories in Agile development?**
- A. Capturing technical details about interfaces
- B. Describing system architecture
- C. Centering development around user needs and outcomes
- D. Listing data constraints and dependencies  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 7
**What do use cases describe?**
- A. System interfaces only
- B. The sequence of interactions between an actor and the system
- C. The company’s business objectives
- D. Only the visual design of the system  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 8
**What role do acceptance criteria play in software requirements?**
- A. They define completion and test conditions for user stories
- B. They replace the need for user testing
- C. They define database schemas
- D. They are used only in documentation reviews  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 9
**What is meant by the statement “Ambiguity dies in acceptance criteria”?**
- A. Writing acceptance criteria helps remove unclear requirements
- B. Ambiguity is unavoidable in software projects
- C. Acceptance criteria make software design simpler
- D. Acceptance criteria are always ambiguous  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 10
**How do the different requirement documents (BRD, SRS, stories, use cases, acceptance criteria) fit together?**
- A. They are competing formats for the same information
- B. They provide complementary perspectives that link business goals to testable software behavior
- C. They are redundant and should not all be used
- D. They apply only to large enterprise projects  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B
