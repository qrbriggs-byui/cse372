Here are **10 multiple-choice questions** based on the attached reading, *How to Participate in Backlog Grooming to Refine and Prioritize User Stories*.
Each question includes four possible answers (A–D) and the correct answer key.

---

### **1.** What is the primary goal of backlog grooming (also called backlog refinement)?

A. To remove all completed tasks from the backlog
B. To review, clarify, and prioritize upcoming work items
C. To finalize sprint goals for the team
D. To assign work to each developer
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **2.** In Agile, what is a **sprint**?

A. A short, fixed period of time to deliver specific work items
B. A team meeting for daily updates
C. A tool used for prioritizing work
D. A product testing phase
✅ **Answer:** A

---

### **3.** What is the correct format for writing a user story?

A. “As a [developer], I want [feature] so that [it meets business goals].”
B. “As a [type of user], I want [some goal] so that [some reason or benefit].”
C. “If [condition], then [action] will occur.”
D. “Build [feature] using [technology].”
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **4.** Who typically leads a backlog grooming session?

A. The Scrum Master
B. The Development Lead
C. The Product Owner
D. The QA Engineer
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **5.** What is the main purpose of user stories in Agile?

A. To serve as fixed, unchangeable requirements
B. To define test cases for each feature
C. To describe features from the end user’s perspective and spark discussion
D. To provide detailed technical documentation
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **6.** During grooming, what should the team ensure about user stories?

A. They are clearly written, properly sized, and prioritized
B. They are completed before sprint planning begins
C. They are rewritten as technical tasks
D. They all have the same level of effort
✅ **Answer:** A

---

### **7.** Which of the following frameworks is mentioned as a prioritization technique in backlog grooming?

A. Kanban
B. MoSCoW
C. RACI
D. Gantt Chart
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **8.** What does the “Value vs. Effort” chart help teams do?

A. Identify who is responsible for each user story
B. Visualize which features are quick wins versus high-effort tasks
C. Track sprint progress over time
D. Estimate the budget for each sprint
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **9.** How can new engineers best contribute to backlog grooming?

A. By observing silently to avoid slowing down the meeting
B. By providing clarifying questions, technical insights, and dependency notes
C. By estimating every story independently
D. By focusing only on documentation
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **10.** What is one major benefit of regular backlog grooming?

A. It allows teams to skip sprint planning
B. It ensures the backlog stays clear, relevant, and prioritized
C. It guarantees that all tasks are small and simple
D. It reduces the need for collaboration
✅ **Answer:** B
