### **1.** What is the main difference between traditional and Agile approaches to documenting requirements?

A. Agile documentation is more detailed and rigid than traditional documentation.
B. Traditional documentation focuses on collaboration, while Agile focuses on formal approval.
C. Agile documentation is lightweight, flexible, and encourages collaboration.
D. Traditional documentation evolves through discussion and change.
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **2.** What does it mean that Agile requirements are *living artifacts*?

A. They are long-term documents that never change.
B. They evolve through ongoing discussion, feedback, and adaptation.
C. They are automatically generated by software tools.
D. They are stored permanently in a centralized database.
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **3.** What does the “INVEST” acronym help teams do?

A. Prioritize the product backlog.
B. Refine user stories so they are ready for development.
C. Estimate project budgets and costs.
D. Manage dependencies between different epics.
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **4.** According to the INVEST criteria, what does the “I” stand for?

A. Incremental
B. Independent
C. Iterative
D. Important
✅ **Answer:** B

### **5.** What is an **epic** in Agile documentation?

A. A small, independent user story completed in one sprint.
B. A collection of related projects.
C. A large user story that represents a major feature and can be broken down into smaller stories.
D. A test case that defines the acceptance criteria for a story.
✅ **Answer:** C

### **6.** Which of the following best describes **acceptance criteria**?

A. Business goals defined by stakeholders at the start of a project.
B. Technical specifications for developers to follow.
C. Conditions that define when a user story is complete and acceptable.
D. The team’s internal testing checklist.
✅ **Answer:** C

### **7.** What format is often used to write acceptance tests?

A. If/Then statements
B. Given/When/Then format
C. Step-by-step task lists
D. Simple bullet points

### **8.** How do epics, user stories, and acceptance tests work together?

### **9.** What is one advantage of documenting requirements using Agile-friendly forms?
