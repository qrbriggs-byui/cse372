# Reading 3.5 – Developing Facilitation and Consensus-Building Skills  
### Multiple Choice Questions

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the reading?  
A. Technical skills are more important than collaboration skills  
B. Facilitation and consensus-building are key to effective teamwork  
C. Teams should avoid disagreement to maintain efficiency  
D. Leadership means making all decisions yourself  
**Answer:** B  

---

**2.** What does the author compare a facilitator to?  
A. A coach calling all the plays  
B. A conductor guiding an orchestra  
C. A teacher grading performance  
D. A manager enforcing rules  
**Answer:** B  

---

**3.** According to the reading, what is facilitation primarily about?  
A. Having all the right answers  
B. Creating conditions for others to contribute meaningfully  
C. Controlling the pace and outcome of meetings  
D. Taking sides to speed up decision-making  
**Answer:** B  

---

**4.** In the design review example, what role does the facilitator play?  
A. Deciding which teammate’s idea wins  
B. Mediating conflict and helping the group find balance  
C. Eliminating differing opinions to reach agreement faster  
D. Setting deadlines for each discussion topic  
**Answer:** B  

---

**5.** What does *consensus-building* mean in this context?  
A. Getting everyone to agree completely on every point  
B. Ensuring that everyone feels heard and can support the final decision  
C. Voting until a majority chooses the final option  
D. Avoiding disagreement to maintain harmony  
**Answer:** B  

---

**6.** In the framework selection example, what helped the team reach consensus?  
A. Listing each member’s top priorities to find shared values  
B. Letting the manager make the final decision  
C. Focusing only on the fastest solution  
D. Ignoring differences to save time  
**Answer:** A  

---

**7.** Which of the following is *not* one of the recommended habits for effective facilitation?  
A. Listening actively  
B. Staying neutral  
C. Summarizing and reframing  
D. Pushing your own preferred outcome  
**Answer:** D  

---

**8.** What does “acknowledging emotion” help achieve during tense discussions?  
A. It makes disagreements disappear  
B. It helps calm frustration and refocus the group productively  
C. It allows facilitators to take sides more effectively  
D. It prevents others from speaking further  
**Answer:** B  

---

**9.** Why is inviting quieter voices important in group discussions?  
A. It speeds up decision-making  
B. It ensures diverse perspectives and encourages inclusion  
C. It avoids conflict by focusing only on talkative members  
D. It limits participation to those with expertise  
**Answer:** B  

---

**10.** According to the author, why are facilitation and consensus-building valuable for future engineers?  
A. They make you seem more authoritative  
B. They help you become someone others enjoy working with  
C. They allow you to avoid collaboration altogether  
D. They eliminate the need for leadership  
**Answer:** B
