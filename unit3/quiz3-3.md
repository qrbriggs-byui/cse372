# Reading 3.3 – Documenting High-Level Needs in a Business Requirements Document (BRD)  
### Multiple Choice Questions

---

**1.** What is the primary purpose of a Business Requirements Document (BRD)?  
A. To define technical architecture and implementation details  
B. To connect business goals with the technical work needed to achieve them  
C. To manage developer schedules and budgets  
D. To serve as a substitute for user manuals  
**Answer:** B  

---

**2.** What key question does the BRD aim to answer?  
A. “How will the software be built?”  
B. “What technologies will we use?”  
C. “What problem are we solving, and what does success look like?”  
D. “Who will maintain the software after deployment?”  
**Answer:** C  

---

**3.** How does a BRD differ from an SRS or FRD?  
A. The BRD focuses on *what* and *why*, while the SRS/FRD focuses on *how*  
B. The BRD includes only design diagrams  
C. The BRD replaces all technical documentation  
D. The BRD is less formal and used only for internal communication  
**Answer:** A  

---

**4.** What is meant by a “high-level need” in the context of a BRD?  
A. A detailed description of system algorithms  
B. A strategic statement describing what stakeholders want the system to achieve  
C. A specific design solution or technology choice  
D. A cost estimate for project implementation  
**Answer:** B  

---

**5.** In the example of the campus parking system, what mistake did the student team make?  
A. They ignored user interface design  
B. They failed to test the system before deployment  
C. They began coding before understanding the true business problem  
D. They didn’t use modern development tools  
**Answer:** C  

---

**6.** Why is defining project scope in the BRD important?  
A. It identifies the programming languages that must be used  
B. It prevents “scope creep” by clarifying what’s included and excluded  
C. It helps estimate server requirements  
D. It ensures that only developers can make decisions  
**Answer:** B  

---

**7.** Which section of the BRD identifies the people or groups involved in the project and their roles?  
A. Assumptions and Constraints  
B. Stakeholders  
C. Business Objectives  
D. High-Level Requirements  
**Answer:** B  

---

**8.** Which section would you use to describe measurable indicators of project success?  
A. Business Objectives  
B. Success Criteria  
C. Scope  
D. High-Level Requirements  
**Answer:** B  

---

**9.** What is the main benefit of documenting assumptions and constraints in a BRD?  
A. It helps reduce the number of meetings  
B. It clarifies limitations and external factors that affect the project  
C. It serves as a backup for technical documentation  
D. It defines user interface elements  
**Answer:** B  

---

**10.** Why is a BRD considered a communication tool rather than just paperwork?  
A. It’s meant to replace project meetings  
B. It allows technical teams to make independent decisions  
C. It ensures shared understanding between business stakeholders and technical teams  
D. It focuses only on internal policies  
**Answer:** C
