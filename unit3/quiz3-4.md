# Reading 3.4 – Connecting Workshop Results to Organizational Goals and Constraints  
### Multiple Choice Questions

---

**1.** What is the main purpose of connecting workshop results to organizational goals and constraints?  
A. To ensure the project stays within budget only  
B. To make sure the ideas align with business priorities and realistic limits  
C. To increase the number of brainstorming ideas  
D. To document all user opinions without evaluation  
**Answer:** B  

---

**2.** What are *organizational goals*?  
A. Daily operational tasks  
B. High-level objectives that define what success looks like for the organization  
C. Detailed technical specifications for a system  
D. Employee performance targets  
**Answer:** B  

---

**3.** Which of the following best defines *constraints* in a project?  
A. Optional ideas that can be added later  
B. External factors unrelated to the project scope  
C. The limits within which a project must operate, such as budget, time, or technology  
D. Personal preferences of the development team  
**Answer:** C  

---

**4.** According to the reading, why can constraints be helpful rather than limiting?  
A. They allow developers to ignore user needs  
B. They prevent creativity from being used in the project  
C. They focus creativity and help ensure achievable design outcomes  
D. They make the process more bureaucratic  
**Answer:** C  

---

**5.** In the mobile app example, why was the “real-time chat” idea a poor fit?  
A. It wasn’t technically feasible  
B. It didn’t improve user experience  
C. It conflicted with the organization’s goal to reduce operational costs  
D. It violated legal requirements  
**Answer:** C  

---

**6.** What is the benefit of mapping workshop ideas to goals and constraints, as shown in the student portal example?  
A. It helps visualize which ideas best align with organizational strategy  
B. It ensures every idea gets implemented  
C. It ranks ideas based on popularity alone  
D. It replaces the need for stakeholder review  
**Answer:** A  

---

**7.** In the nonprofit example, what change did the team make to align with the organization’s goals?  
A. They removed all reporting features  
B. They built a simpler system that exported data to a spreadsheet instead of creating a custom dashboard  
C. They added advanced AI analytics  
D. They extended the project timeline for more development  
**Answer:** B  

---

**8.** Which of the following is *not* one of the recommended tips for connecting workshop results to organizational goals?  
A. Revisit the mission statement  
B. Ask “why” and “how” to evaluate ideas  
C. Prioritize ideas that show measurable impact  
D. Ignore constraints to encourage creativity  
**Answer:** D  

---

**9.** What is a key advantage of documenting how workshop results align with goals?  
A. It allows teams to justify design decisions and maintain focus on strategy  
B. It reduces the need for user testing  
C. It eliminates the need for approval  
D. It ensures every idea is implemented exactly as proposed  
**Answer:** A  

---

**10.** According to the author, what is the hallmark of an effective engineer or problem-solver?  
A. The ability to code faster than others  
B. The ability to turn creative ideas into practical, goal-aligned solutions  
C. The ability to manage budgets independently  
D. The ability to avoid constraints altogether  
**Answer:** B
