# Quiz: Designing and Deploying User Surveys that Actually Work

This quiz covers key concepts from the reading on designing and deploying user surveys for meaningful, statistically relevant feedback.

---

**1.** What is the main purpose of a user survey in the requirements gathering process?  
A. To test software performance  
B. To gather insights into user needs, motivations, and frustrations  
C. To replace interviews and focus groups  
D. To promote a product to users  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**2.** What does *meaningful feedback* refer to in the context of user surveys?  
A. Data that can be visualized easily  
B. Responses that reveal genuine insights into user needs and motivations  
C. Feedback from internal team members only  
D. Quantitative data gathered from a small group  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**3.** What does *statistically relevant feedback* mean?  
A. Responses that are easy to summarize in charts  
B. Data from a large and diverse enough sample to draw reliable conclusions  
C. Qualitative data from interviews  
D. Feedback from users who responded first  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**4.** Why is it important to start a survey with clear objectives?  
A. It helps you focus on the right questions and avoid unnecessary ones  
B. It allows you to make the survey longer  
C. It makes the survey look more professional  
D. It ensures every user answers all questions  
**→ Correct answer:** A  

---

**5.** Which type of survey question provides *quantitative data*?  
A. Open-ended questions  
B. Questions that invite storytelling  
C. Closed-ended questions such as multiple choice or rating scales  
D. Follow-up interview questions  
**→ Correct answer:** C  

---

**6.** What kind of information do open-ended questions provide?  
A. Numeric ratings that can be averaged  
B. Descriptive, qualitative insights from users’ own words  
C. Information that is difficult to interpret  
D. Simple yes/no answers  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**7.** According to the reading, what is the ideal length for a user survey?  
A. 15–20 questions that take 10–15 minutes  
B. 10–15 questions that take about 8 minutes  
C. 5–10 questions that take under five minutes to answer  
D. As many questions as necessary to cover all features  
**→ Correct answer:** C  

---

**8.** What is the term for the group of people that your survey should represent?  
A. Survey respondents  
B. Data sample  
C. Target population  
D. Beta testers  
**→ Correct answer:** C  

---

**9.** Why should you include both open-ended and closed-ended questions in a survey?  
A. To balance measurable trends with rich contextual insights  
B. To make the survey appear more professional  
C. To increase the total number of questions  
D. To make data analysis more complex  
**→ Correct answer:** A  

---

**10.** What is the key to presenting survey findings effectively?  
A. Use only numerical charts for clarity  
B. Share data privately within the development team  
C. Combine visualized data with user quotes to create empathy and understanding  
D. Focus on the most positive results only  
**→ Correct answer:** C  

---
