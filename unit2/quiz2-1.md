# Quiz 2-1: How to Conduct Structured and Unstructured Stakeholder Interviews

## Question 1
**What is the main purpose of stakeholder interviews in requirements gathering?**
- A. To test new software features
- B. To understand how work is really done and uncover true needs
- C. To collect marketing data
- D. To evaluate team performance  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 2
**What is the main characteristic of a structured interview?**
- A. The interviewer follows a flexible, open-ended conversation
- B. Every participant is asked different questions
- C. The interviewer uses a consistent script and question order
- D. Questions change depending on the stakeholder’s role  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 3
**What is the primary advantage of unstructured interviews?**
- A. They ensure identical responses across all participants
- B. They help uncover hidden knowledge and context through open conversation
- C. They save time by avoiding tangents
- D. They prevent bias completely  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 4
**According to the reading, when should you choose a structured interview format?**
- A. When exploring a completely new domain
- B. When you need comparable data across many roles
- C. When you want spontaneous stories
- D. When you have no time to prepare questions  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 5
**What is a good rule of thumb mentioned for conducting interviews?**
- A. Use structure to ensure coverage; use curiosity to uncover context
- B. Always stick rigidly to the script
- C. Keep interviews under ten minutes
- D. Avoid asking follow-up questions  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 6
**What is a benefit of semi-structured interviews?**
- A. They combine consistency with flexibility for deeper discovery
- B. They remove the need for follow-up questions
- C. They limit conversation to predefined questions only
- D. They eliminate interviewer bias completely  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 7
**Which of the following is a recommended best practice during structured interviews?**
- A. Ask leading questions to guide answers
- B. Use neutral wording and clarify terminology
- C. Skip unrelated tangents without acknowledging them
- D. Focus only on yes/no questions  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 8
**What is the main goal of active listening during stakeholder interviews?**
- A. To capture exact quotes for reports
- B. To ensure stakeholders feel heard and understood, revealing real needs
- C. To complete the interview faster
- D. To validate your assumptions early  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 9
**After the interview, what is one recommended follow-up step?**
- A. Delete your notes for privacy reasons
- B. Wait a week before documenting insights
- C. Send a thank-you message and share how their input will be used
- D. Immediately begin coding based on initial impressions  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 10
**What is the key difference between structured and unstructured interviews?**
- A. Structured interviews are casual; unstructured ones are rigid
- B. Structured interviews use consistency, while unstructured ones rely on conversational flow
- C. Structured interviews require multiple interviewers
- D. Unstructured interviews are only for testing software prototypes  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B
