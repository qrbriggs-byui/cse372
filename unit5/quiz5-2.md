Here are **10 multiple-choice questions** based on the attached reading, *Using Sprint Planning to Uncover Hidden Dependencies and Refine Acceptance Criteria.*
Each question includes four options (A–D) and the correct answer key.

---

### **1.** What is the main purpose of a Sprint Planning meeting in Scrum?

A. To design the overall software architecture
B. To decide which user stories will be worked on and how they’ll be completed
C. To review the previous sprint’s performance metrics
D. To assign tasks to individual developers
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **2.** What is produced at the end of a Sprint Planning meeting?

A. A finalized product design document
B. A Sprint Backlog that outlines agreed-upon work for the sprint
C. A list of unresolved issues from the previous sprint
D. A report on team performance
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **3.** What is a “dependency” in the context of software development?

A. A part of the software that depends on user feedback
B. A relationship where one task or system relies on another to proceed
C. A testing technique used to ensure software stability
D. A feature that must be removed before deployment
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **4.** Why are hidden dependencies especially problematic?

A. They tend to add unnecessary features to the product
B. They increase sprint velocity and shorten delivery time
C. They can cause delays or blockers if not discovered early
D. They simplify the team’s workload by grouping tasks together
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **5.** What are **acceptance criteria**?

A. The project’s overall budget constraints
B. The coding standards for each development task
C. The conditions that must be met for a user story to be considered complete
D. The test cases written by the QA team after coding is finished
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **6.** What is the benefit of refining acceptance criteria during Sprint Planning?

A. It allows teams to estimate task durations more accurately
B. It clarifies expectations and reduces rework later in the sprint
C. It helps the Scrum Master finalize resource allocation
D. It ensures that only developers contribute to story planning
✅ **Answer:** B

---

### **7.** Which of the following is **not** listed as a strategy for spotting dependencies?

A. Walking through each story as a team
B. Using a dependency board or diagram
C. Waiting until the end of the sprint to review blockers
D. Asking, “What could block this story?”
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **8.** In the example about the password reset feature, which of the following was **one of the acceptance criteria**?

A. The user receives a reset link that expires after 24 hours
B. The system sends daily reminders to reset the password
C. The password is automatically changed every 30 days
D. The user can skip the password reset step if they choose
✅ **Answer:** A

---

### **9.** What is the role of multiple perspectives (developers, testers, designers) during Sprint Planning?

A. To speed up decision-making by minimizing discussion
B. To ensure only the Product Owner defines all requirements
C. To provide different viewpoints that help uncover risks and dependencies
D. To separate technical work from design and testing discussions
✅ **Answer:** C

---

### **10.** According to the reading, what is the ultimate benefit of well-run Sprint Planning sessions?

A. They reduce collaboration by assigning clear ownership
B. They transform planning from a meeting into a collaboration tool
C. They eliminate the need for sprint reviews
D. They ensure no changes are made during the sprint
✅ **Answer:** B
