# Quiz 2-1: How to Conduct Observations or Job Shadowing to Capture Workflows and Tacit Requirements

## Question 1
**What is the main purpose of conducting observations or job shadowing in software projects?**
- A. To evaluate employee performance
- B. To identify undocumented, real-world workflows and tacit requirements
- C. To replace interviews entirely
- D. To train users on new systems  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 2
**What key insight did the author gain during their first warehouse observation?**
- A. Employees were misusing the barcode scanners
- B. Much of the real work involved undocumented actions and interactions
- C. The system already captured all important workflows
- D. Observations are less useful than surveys  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 3
**Why can interviews sometimes miss important information?**
- A. People intentionally hide workflow details
- B. People forget to describe second-nature behaviors
- C. Interviews are usually too short
- D. Interviewers take poor notes  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 4
**What should you always do before starting an observation?**
- A. Begin recording without notice
- B. Ask participants not to talk during work
- C. Obtain permission and explain your purpose
- D. Shadow only senior staff members  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 5
**Why is it useful to observe both experts and newcomers?**
- A. Experts make fewer mistakes
- B. Newcomers follow official documentation more closely
- C. They provide different perspectives on the same system
- D. It doubles your data collection time  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 6
**What is a best practice while observing people at work?**
- A. Interrupt often to clarify steps
- B. Blend in quietly and avoid disrupting their workflow
- C. Sit beside them and ask continuous questions
- D. Record only verbal explanations  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 7
**Which of the following should you capture in observation notes?**
- A. Only system interactions
- B. Observations of both actions and environment
- C. Opinions from managers
- D. Personal evaluations of employees  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 8
**What are tacit requirements?**
- A. Requirements already documented in manuals
- B. Implicit behaviors, signals, or norms users rarely articulate
- C. Security features defined by management
- D. Strictly technical system constraints  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 9
**What should you do after completing an observation session?**
- A. Wait several weeks before reviewing notes
- B. Immediately start designing solutions
- C. Debrief and summarize key workflows, pain points, and patterns
- D. Keep findings confidential and unshared  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 10
**What does the author mean by “Observation isn’t about catching errors; it’s about catching reality”?**
- A. Observation should focus on identifying user mistakes
- B. The goal is to understand authentic work practices, not to judge performance
- C. Observers should correct errors immediately
- D. Observation replaces testing and QA  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B
