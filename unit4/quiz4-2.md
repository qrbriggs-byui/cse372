# Quiz: Building Low-Fidelity Prototypes to Clarify Requirements

This quiz tests your understanding of how low-fidelity prototypes support requirements gathering and communication in software development.

---

**1.** What is the main purpose of a low-fidelity prototype?  
A. To present a polished, final version of a product  
B. To explore structure, flow, and functionality in an early, simplified form  
C. To showcase visual design and branding choices  
D. To demonstrate completed code functionality  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**2.** What is another term for a low-fidelity prototype?  
A. Mock production model  
B. Lo-fi prototype  
C. Mid-fidelity wireframe  
D. Full interface prototype  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**3.** What are **wireframes** used for in low-fidelity prototyping?  
A. Showing detailed color and typography choices  
B. Outlining where key elements like buttons and menus will appear on a screen  
C. Collecting user analytics data  
D. Demonstrating final animations  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**4.** How do **clickable mockups** differ from wireframes?  
A. They are fully coded and functional  
B. They include interactivity, allowing users to navigate between screens  
C. They are created only using paper and pencil  
D. They are more static and less user-friendly than wireframes  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**5.** Why are low-fidelity prototypes particularly effective for gathering feedback?  
A. Users are more comfortable giving honest feedback on unfinished designs  
B. They hide design flaws from users  
C. They eliminate the need for usability testing  
D. They replace the need for developer discussions  
**→ Correct answer:** A  

---

**6.** Which of the following is the *first step* in building a low-fidelity prototype?  
A. Adding colors and branding  
B. Writing test cases  
C. Starting with user goals and main tasks  
D. Coding the backend logic  
**→ Correct answer:** C  

---

**7.** What tool type or material can be used to create a low-fidelity prototype?  
A. Figma or Balsamiq  
B. Photoshop only  
C. Spreadsheet software  
D. Database tools  
**→ Correct answer:** A  

---

**8.** In the example of the campus study room app, what feedback might users provide during testing?  
A. “It’s not clear how to change the reservation time.”  
B. “The logo color should be darker.”  
C. “The code needs optimization.”  
D. “The app should include financial tracking.”  
**→ Correct answer:** A  

---

**9.** What is the key benefit of testing a low-fidelity prototype early?  
A. It reduces user engagement  
B. It helps identify misunderstandings before development begins  
C. It speeds up coding of the final product  
D. It confirms that the design is visually appealing  
**→ Correct answer:** B  

---

**10.** According to the reading, what is the true value of low-fidelity prototypes?  
A. Their visual appeal and accuracy  
B. The conversations and collaboration they inspire among stakeholders  
C. Their ability to serve as final deliverables  
D. Their role in software deployment  
**→ Correct answer:** B
