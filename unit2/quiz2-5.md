# Quiz 2-5: How to Apply Active Listening and Note-Taking Skills to Capture Accurate Requirements

## Question 1
**What is the main idea of the reading?**
- A. Writing long reports ensures project success
- B. Active listening and effective note-taking lead to accurate, actionable requirements
- C. Meetings should always be recorded instead of taking notes
- D. Most clients prefer quick summaries instead of detailed documentation  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 2
**What does active listening involve according to the reading?**
- A. Waiting for your turn to speak
- B. Focusing only on technical details
- C. Being fully present and understanding meaning, emotion, and intent
- D. Writing down everything word-for-word without thinking  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 3
**In the example of the stakeholder who wanted updates every second, what did active listening reveal?**
- A. They actually needed updates every minute, not every second
- B. The feature was unnecessary
- C. They wanted a completely new dashboard
- D. The stakeholder didn’t understand the system’s limits  
✅ **Correct Answer:** A

---

## Question 4
**Which of the following is NOT a recommended practice for active listening?**
- A. Paraphrasing and confirming what you heard
- B. Listening for emotions and frustrations
- C. Interrupting often to save time
- D. Allowing silence to invite more details  
✅ **Correct Answer:** C

---

## Question 5
**What is the main purpose of paraphrasing during requirements conversations?**
- A. To show off technical knowledge
- B. To catch misunderstandings early and build trust
- C. To fill time in the conversation
- D. To summarize only at the end of the meeting  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 6
**What should good notes include according to the reading?**
- A. Perfect grammar and formatting
- B. Clear, organized information that’s useful later
- C. Only high-level summaries without quotes
- D. Developer-focused language  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 7
**Why should you capture quotes verbatim in your notes?**
- A. They make documentation longer
- B. They preserve the stakeholder’s authentic voice and emotion
- C. They replace the need for summaries
- D. They show that you recorded every word accurately  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 8
**What does separating facts from interpretations help prevent?**
- A. Typing errors
- B. Personal bias and incorrect assumptions
- C. Overly detailed notes
- D. Duplicate documentation  
✅ **Correct Answer:** B

---

## Question 9
**What should you do immediately after a meeting?**
- A. File your notes and move to the next task
- B. Review, clean up, and share a summary for confirmation
- C. Ask participants for new meeting times
- D. Wait until the next day to rewrite your notes

## Question 10
**According to the reading, why are active listening and note-taking called “superpowers”?**
