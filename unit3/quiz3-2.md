# Reading 3.2 – Using Brainstorming and Affinity Mapping to Cluster Requirement Themes  
### Multiple Choice Questions

---

**1.** What is the main goal of brainstorming in a requirements workshop?  
A. To evaluate and refine ideas immediately  
B. To generate as many ideas as possible without judgment  
C. To decide on final system requirements  
D. To document user stories and acceptance criteria  
**Answer:** B  

---

**2.** During brainstorming, why should participants “set aside judgment”?  
A. To prevent bias and encourage creative, open idea generation  
B. To avoid taking notes until the end of the session  
C. To save time by skipping discussion  
D. To maintain strict order of speaking  
**Answer:** A  

---

**3.** In the reading’s example of a study planner app, what humorous idea inspired innovation?  
A. “AI that scolds you for procrastinating”  
B. “Color-coded sticky notes”  
C. “Offline mode for studying in the library”  
D. “Built-in grade calculator”  
**Answer:** A  

---

**4.** What tool is commonly used to capture ideas quickly during brainstorming?  
A. Wireframes  
B. Sticky notes or digital boards like Miro or Jamboard  
C. UML diagrams  
D. Gantt charts  
**Answer:** B  

---

**5.** What is *affinity mapping* used for after brainstorming?  
A. To rank features based on cost  
B. To organize similar ideas into themes or clusters  
C. To create a project schedule  
D. To write formal requirement specifications  
**Answer:** B  

---

**6.** In the tutoring platform example, what unexpected insight emerged from affinity mapping?  
A. The need for better scheduling tools  
B. The importance of user interface design  
C. The theme of trust between tutors and students  
D. The lack of mobile compatibility  
**Answer:** C  

---

**7.** How do brainstorming and affinity mapping complement each other?  
A. Brainstorming gathers data, while affinity mapping creates prototypes  
B. Brainstorming focuses on creativity, while affinity mapping provides structure  
C. Both focus exclusively on project budgeting  
D. Brainstorming replaces the need for group collaboration  
**Answer:** B  

---

**8.** According to the reading, what should facilitators do to ensure success during brainstorming?  
A. Set a clear goal and encourage participation from everyone  
B. Limit idea generation to experienced members  
C. Stick to a rigid agenda and time limit  
D. Avoid visual tools to reduce distraction  
**Answer:** A  

---

**9.** What should teams do after forming clusters during affinity mapping?  
A. Label each group with a meaningful theme and reflect on what it reveals  
B. Vote on which cluster to delete  
C. Immediately translate clusters into code  
D. Merge all clusters into one large category  
**Answer:** A  

---

**10.** What is the ultimate purpose of using brainstorming and affinity mapping together in requirements gathering?  
A. To collect as much data as possible, regardless of quality  
B. To move from scattered ideas to shared understanding and insight  
C. To replace stakeholder interviews  
D. To finalize system architecture  
**Answer:** B
